## Side Projects

I've started toying around with the idea of taking my knowledge of SOC 2 / ISO 27001 compliance programs and packaging it into an open-source CLI tool. Years ago I discovered Comply by StrongDM when I was looking for prior art on writing up internal policies and controls for a startup seeking SOC 2 attestation. It was a nifty little tool, but felt very underbaked and I'm not surprised it's [effectively abandonware at this point](https://github.com/strongdm/comply/issues/133). I have Opinions about this whole field and how the SaaS/commercial vendors are largely selling security theater by pitching early stage startups on claims like "SOC 2 audit ready in hours". I firmly believe that the true value of a formal compliance program lies in the improved processes and controls a company can implement, not just the audit. And I'm excited to build a tool that helps companies make these improvements in an intentional and sustainable way.

## Reading

Currently reading The Great Hunt by Robert Jordan. February was a bad brain month that tied me up in work so I didn't make much progress toward the goal of finishing the Wheel of Time series in 2026. I suspect I'll catch up this month and plan to finish this book and The Dragon Reborn before April. I'm traveling at least two (if not three) weeks in April which means I'll have lots of airport time to power through my Kobo.

I remain a fervent user of [Libby](https://help.libbyapp.com/en-us/6144.htm?ref=purdy.dev) and have perfected LibraryMaxxing through the [Metropolitan Interlibrary Exchange program](https://multcolib.org/library-account-basics/library-card-options?ref=purdy.dev) that allows me to have six library cards each with their own holds and loans. Also, I'll continue to plug [Queer Liberation Library](https://www.queerliberationlibrary.org/?ref=purdy.dev) as a wonderful free digital library resource that opens applications to anyone with a U.S. mailing address. One of the rare perks of having a U.S. mailing address in these Times.

New for March I've streamlined how I read web articles and email newsletters. I sync my Kobo to a local digital library running on a home server. I've written a little Go server that lets me paste web URLs or forward emails to an email address and the program automatically converts the content into an EPUB and adds it to my digital library with tagging and metadata so that my Kobo picks it up on the next sync. This has made reading long form premium articles like [Ed Zitron's newsletter](https://www.wheresyoured.at/?ref=purdy.dev) much easier since I'd rather have them on my Kobo than on a cursed phone or computer screen.
